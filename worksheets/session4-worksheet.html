✓ Answer Key — Session 4
A — Iteration Counts
A1: 5 times, i = 1,2,3,4,5  |  A2: 6 times, i = 0,2,4,6,8,10  |  A3: 5 times, i = 5,4,3,2,1  |  A4: 1 time, i = 3
A4 is the interesting one — when start equals stop, the loop runs exactly once. A3 requires the -1 step; without it, the loop wouldn't run at all (Lua won't count up from 5 to 1 automatically).
B — While Trace
Iter 1: 80 → 55  |  Iter 2: 55 → 30  |  Iter 3: 30 → 5  |  Loop ends. Final: mana = 5
After iteration 3, mana is 5. The condition mana >= 25 is checked again: 5 >= 25 is false, so the loop ends. The loop ran 3 times, not 4.
C1 — Infinite Loop
Problem: health is never modified inside the loop, so the condition never becomes false.
local health = 100
while health > 0 do
  health = health - 10   -- must decrease for loop to end
end
C2 — Missing Step
Problem: counting from 10 to 1 requires a negative step. Without it, Lua defaults to +1, and since 10 is already past 1, the loop runs 0 times.
for i = 10, 1, -1 do
  -- Countdown display
end
D — Wave Score Trace
i=1 (odd): +10 = 10  |  i=2 (even): +20 = 30  |  i=3 (odd): +10 = 40  |  i=4 (even): +20 = 60  |  i=5 (odd): +10 = 70  |  i=6 (even): +20 = 90
Final total = 90. The % 2 == 0 check identifies even numbers (2, 4, 6) and awards double points. There are 3 odd iterations (10 each = 30) and 3 even iterations (20 each = 60), for a total of 90.
E1 — Sample Answer
local dotDamage = 0
local tickDamage = 12

for i = 1, 5 do
  dotDamage = dotDamage + tickDamage
end
-- dotDamage = 60 after 5 ticks
A for loop is the right choice here because the number of ticks (5) is known in advance.
E2 — Sample Answer
local attempts = 0
local maxAttempts = 10

while attempts < maxAttempts do
  attempts = attempts + 1
  -- Check spawn point validity...
end
The while loop is appropriate when we don't know how many valid checks will be needed, only when to stop. Note: the loop could also stop early with a break statement (covered in advanced sessions).
E3 — Sample Answer
Use for when the count is fixed; use while when the count depends on something that changes at runtime.
for example: Applying a buff to exactly 4 party members. You know the count.   while example: Keeping a door locked until a player's score crosses a threshold. You don't know how many rounds it will take.
